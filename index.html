Research

I’m a Master’s student in Computer Science at the Instituto de Ciências Matemáticas e de Computação (ICMC) at the University of São Paulo, specializing in machine learning and data mining under the guidance of Prof. Andre Carlos Ponce de Leon Ferreira de Carvalho.

I’m currently interning at the Machine Learning Department at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Prof. Leila Wehbe, focusing on using graph neural networks and fMRI to identify autism-related symptoms.

I hold a degree in Mechatronic Engineering from the University of São Paulo, where I engaged in various research projects. Notably, I collaborated remotely with the iProBe Lab at Michigan State University under Prof. Arun Ross and with Prof. Raimundo Nonato and Prof. Luis Gustavo Nonato at USP. My work involved generating fake fingerprints using GANs and autoencoders, and analyzing adversarial examples.
