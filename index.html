Research

I'm a Computer Science Master's Student at the Instituto de Ciências Matemáticas e de Computação (ICMC) from the University of São Paulo, focusing on machine learning and data mining, advised by Prof. Andre Carlos Ponce de Leon Ferreira de Carvalho. Currently, I am doing a research internship at the Machine Learning Department at Carnegie Mellon University under the supervision of Prof. Leila Wehbe. My current research is on identifying autism-related symptoms using graph neural networks and fMRI.

I graduated in Mechatronic Engineering from the University of São Paulo, where I had the opportunity to conduct some research projects. For two years, I conducted online collaborative research with the iProBe Lab at Michigan State University under the supervision of Prof. Arun Ross and under the supervision of Prof. Raimundo Nonato and Prof. Luis Gustavo Nonato from the University of São Paulo. During this research, I worked on generating fake fingerprints using GANs and autoencoders, and analyzed adversarial examples with mathematical singularity tools.

My research interests center around tackling efficiency problems in machine learning from systems, algorithms, and data perspectives. In my head, the big goal is to drive accessibility and participation in the creation of the AI technologies we use so that resources cease to be a bottleneck or criteria for being part of the decision-making process.

Teaching Experience.
